Introduction

Your genes are the blueprint you were born with, but they are not your destiny. While genetic variations (SNPs) can predispose you to certain tendencies, how these genes are expressed depends largely on epigenetics—the interaction of diet, lifestyle, stress, toxins, and environment. This report highlights your potential areas of strength and vulnerability, with suggestions for labs, diet, lifestyle, and protective actions that can help you optimize health and prevent disease expression.

Legend for Genetic Findings

• +/+ (red bold): Homozygous variant. Both copies of the gene carry this change. Strongest impact, higher priority for diet & lifestyle support.

• +/– (black): Heterozygous variant. One copy of the gene carries this change. Moderate effect, usually balanced with the right habits.

• –/– (not shown): Typical version. No variant of concern here.

Food and Nutrients

Genes can influence how well you absorb and activate essential vitamins and minerals like folate, B12, vitamin D, magnesium, and selenium. Weaknesses here can drive fatigue, bone loss, or immune issues. By choosing the right foods and nutrient forms, you can bypass your genetic bottlenecks and thrive.

SNPs:

  • MTHFR C677T (rs1801133) [Homozygous +/+]
    This SNP reduces the activity of the MTHFR enzyme by up to 70%, which slows the conversion of folate into 5-methyltetrahydrofolate (active folate). Reduced methylfolate can increase homocysteine levels, affecting cardiovascular health, neurotransmitter synthesis, and DNA methylation.
    Follow-up Labs: Serum homocysteine, methylfolate, riboflavin levels
    Recommended Actions: Supplement with methylated folate (5-MTHF), riboflavin, and methyl-B12; ensure dietary folate intake; monitor homocysteine.
  • VDR (rs2228570, rs1544410) [Heterozygous +/–]
    Variants in the vitamin D receptor gene may reduce receptor function, limiting vitamin D signaling in bone, immune, and metabolic pathways. Even with normal 25(OH)D levels, cellular signaling may be impaired.
    Follow-up Labs: Serum vitamin D, calcium, immune markers
    Recommended Actions: Optimize vitamin D intake; consider D3 supplementation; monitor calcium levels.
  • BCMO1 (rs7501331) [Heterozygous +/–]
    BCMO1 converts beta-carotene to active vitamin A. Variants reduce efficiency, increasing reliance on preformed vitamin A for vision, immunity, and epithelial health.
    Follow-up Labs: Retinol, carotenoid levels
    Recommended Actions: Include carotenoid-rich foods; consider preformed vitamin A supplementation.

Detoxification & Biotransformation

Your body works around the clock to clear toxins, hormones, and environmental chemicals. Genes in detox pathways affect how quickly or efficiently you process these compounds. Understanding these variants allows targeted nutritional and lifestyle interventions to optimize detoxification.

SNPs:

  • COMT V158M (rs4680) [Heterozygous +/–]
    Catechol-O-methyltransferase metabolizes catecholamines and catechol estrogens. Variants reduce activity, increasing oxidative stress and hormone metabolite accumulation. This can exacerbate anxiety, estrogen dominance, and toxin sensitivity.
    Follow-up Labs: Catecholamine and estrogen metabolite panels
    Recommended Actions: Magnesium, methyl donors, and antioxidant support.
  • GSTM1 (deletion) [Heterozygous +/–]
    Glutathione-S-transferase deletion reduces phase II detoxification capacity, limiting removal of xenobiotics and oxidative compounds.
    Follow-up Labs: Glutathione status
    Recommended Actions: NAC, glycine, selenium supplementation.

Mental Health, Neurotransmitters & Stress Resilience

Neurotransmitters like dopamine, serotonin, and GABA determine whether you feel motivated, balanced, and emotionally steady — or scattered, moody, and reactive. Genetic tendencies may influence how quickly you make or clear these brain chemicals.

SNPs:

  • BDNF Val66Met (rs6265) [Heterozygous +/–]
    Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) supports the growth and survival of neurons. Variants can impair neurogenesis, reduce brain plasticity, and contribute to mood disorders like depression and anxiety.
    Follow-up Labs: BDNF levels, mood symptom tracking
    Recommended Actions: Support BDNF levels with physical exercise, omega-3s, and intermittent fasting. Manage stress levels through relaxation techniques.
